Organic Chemistry Reagents for COMEDK

LiAlH4 (Lithium Aluminium Hydride)
- Strong reducing agent; reduces aldehydes, ketones, esters, and carboxylic acids to alcohols
NaBH4 (Sodium Borohydride)
- Milder reducing agent; reduces aldehydes and ketones to alcohols
PCC (Pyridinium chlorochromate)
- Oxidizing agent; oxidizes primary alcohols to aldehydes and secondary alcohols to ketones
KMnO4 (Potassium permanganate)
- Strong oxidizing agent; oxidizes alcohols to carboxylic acids, alkenes to diols or acids
CrO3 (Chromium trioxide)
- Oxidizing agent; Jones reagent with H2SO4, oxidizes alcohols to ketones or acids
O3 (Ozone)
- Ozonolysis; cleaves alkenes to form aldehydes or ketones
H2/Pd, Pt, Ni
- Catalytic hydrogenation; reduces alkenes, alkynes, and nitro groups
Lindlar’s Catalyst
- Poisoned catalyst; reduces alkynes to cis-alkenes
Na/NH3 (Sodium in liquid ammonia)
- Reduces alkynes to trans-alkenes
SOCl2 (Thionyl chloride)
- Converts alcohols to alkyl chlorides
PBr3 (Phosphorus tribromide)
- Converts alcohols to alkyl bromides
H2SO4 (Sulfuric acid)
- Dehydrating agent; forms alkenes from alcohols
AlCl3 (Aluminium chloride)
- Lewis acid; used in Friedel-Crafts reactions
BH3·THF (Borane-tetrahydrofuran)
- Hydroboration-oxidation; converts alkenes to alcohols with anti-Markovnikov selectivity
mCPBA (meta-Chloroperoxybenzoic acid)
- Oxidizes alkenes to epoxides
NaOEt (Sodium ethoxide)
- Strong base; used in E2 eliminations and Claisen condensations

NaNH2 (Sodium amide)
- Very strong base; used for deprotonation and alkyne synthesis
